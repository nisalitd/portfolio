OVERVIEW

VEXEL is an autonomous mobile robotic manipulator designed for disease detection and targeted spraying in confined polytunnel environments. The platform integrates a 4-wheel mobile base for navigation, a 3-DoF Cartesian manipulator for canopy-level positioning, and a 2-DoF gimbal-mounted end effector for orientation-controlled sensing and spraying. Perception is enabled through an RGB + NIR multispectral perception pipeline that supports NDVI-based stress detection, Random Forest classification of bacterial leaf spot, and color-based segmentation of mealybugs. A dual-nozzle spraying unit delivers both upward abaxial coverage and localized or wide-angle foliar treatment. Autonomy is coordinated through a ROS 2 stack combining SLAM Toolbox and Nav2, enabling mapping, waypoint traversal, and plant-wise scan → detect → spray execution without operator intervention.

SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The robot integrates three coordinated subsystems:

4-wheel Mobile Base Navigates narrow aisles, maintains stability over uneven soil
3-DoF Cartesian Manipulator Positions the end effector across the canopy envelope
2-DoF Gimbal End Effector Houses dual cameras + dual nozzles for sensing and spraying

PERCEPTION & DISEASE DETECTION

The perception system uses RGB + NIR multispectral imaging to analyse plant health:

NDVI Detects early-stage stress before visible symptoms
Random Forest
(HOG features)		 Classifies bacterial leaf spot
Color segmentation Identifies mealybug clusters

SPRAYING MECHANISM

Spraying is performed via a dual-nozzle mechanism and triggered automatically when disease is detected during the scan cycle:

Upward-facing multi-orifice Treats undersides of leaves (mealybugs)
Swirl atomizer (adjustable, 30°–120°) Localized disease control and general foliar spraying

CONTROL & AUTONOMY

Navigation ROS 2 Nav2 stack with SLAM-based mapping and waypoint traversal
Scan Cycles Three scan types: Mealybug (upward) • Leaf-spot (bidirectional) • General (uniform)
Pause–Scan-Spray–Resume Robot halts at each plant, executes the scan cycle, triggers spraying if disease is detected, resumes motion
