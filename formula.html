OVERVIEW

This page documents contributions to Team Falcon E Racing, Sri Lanka’s first Formula Student electric vehicle team. As a Vehicle Dynamics and Electronics Engineer, the work spanned full-vehicle simulation, telemetry, and energy systems for the Falcon E2 race car. Responsibilities included developing tire and vehicle models in MATLAB/Simulink for lap-time and handling optimization, implementing a real-time data acquisition and telemetry stack, and leading HV battery pack design and assembly of Sri Lanka’s first 600 V Li-Po traction battery. The work combined system-level integration, on-track validation, and competition-grade documentation across mechanical, electrical, and control domains.

Key Contributions

  • Developed full tire and vehicle models for Falcon E2 using MATLAB/Simulink.
  • Optimized steering geometry for improved handling and lap-time performance.
  • Designed and implemented a multi-channel data acquisition and telemetry system.
  • Contributed to 600 V Li-Po traction battery assembly and HV/LV system integration.
  • Conducted vehicle testing, data analysis, and performance correlation.
  • Prepared competition-grade documentation and cost event BOMs.

Public Outreach

Represented Team Falcon E Racing at national exhibitions including TECHNO 2023/2024, the Colombo EV Motor Show 2024, and Sahasak Nimawum 2025, promoting student innovation in electric mobility.

Key Technical Skills

  • Vehicle and tire modeling (MATLAB / Simulink)
  • Real-time telemetry and data acquisition (DAQ / CAN / Python)
  • Steering geometry optimization and performance tuning
  • High-voltage battery design and assembly (600 V Li-Po)
  • System integration, wiring, and fault isolation (LV / HV)
  • Competition documentation and cost analysis
