Dual-Tunable MEMS Diffraction Grating with Decoupled Pitch and Blaze Angle Control

  • Skills
  • Presentation

OVERVIEW

This project presents a dual-tunable MEMS diffraction grating capable of independent control of pitch and blaze angle for adaptive optical systems. The design introduces a decoupled actuation architecture that combines electrostatic comb-drive pitch tuning with a prism-dimple vertical-field mechanism for blaze control, enabling pure rotational motion while suppressing pull-in instability. Co-moving per-slat electrodes eliminate stiction during translation and permit localized voltage adjustment to maintain uniformity across the array. Finite element simulations in COMSOL verified uniform 0–608 nm pitch tuning at ≤55 V and 0–7.9° blaze tuning at ≤115 V, achieving roughly fourfold improvement over prior dual-mode designs. Modal analysis confirmed high dynamic stiffness (~7.2 kHz) suitable for stable, high-speed optical operation. The structure was optimized for combined bulk–surface micromachining using anisotropic single-crystal silicon and polysilicon layers, supporting scalable fabrication and high diffraction efficiency for tunable spectroscopy and optical communication applications.

KEY TECHNICAL SKILLS

  • Electrostatic actuator design and analytical modeling
  • COMSOL Multiphysics simulation of electro-mechanical coupling
  • Parametric optimization of pitch and blaze actuation geometry
  • Process-level structural modeling for MEMS design validation
