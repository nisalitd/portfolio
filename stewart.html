OVERVIEW

This project presents a rotary-actuated 6-DoF motion platform designed for intuitive, pose-mirroring control using real-time hand tracking. The full-scale design was developed to support the payload of a human occupant, while a reduced-scale prototype was fabricated to experimentally validate the control architecture and motion behavior. The mechanism employs an optimized 6-6 parallel geometry with rotary–spherical–spherical joints, refined through sensitivity and genetic-algorithm analysis to maximize workspace and ensure ergonomic operation. Structural and topology optimization improved stiffness-to-weight performance, while torque analysis under extreme load cases guided actuator selection. Vision-based control extracts 6-DoF hand pose via a CNN tracker and maps it to actuator angles through a custom inverse-kinematics solver supporting three coordinate-mapping modes. A modular MQTT framework and web-based GUI coordinate vision, computation, and actuation, enabling smooth, real-time multi-mode motion for immersive simulation and human-in-the-loop control.
